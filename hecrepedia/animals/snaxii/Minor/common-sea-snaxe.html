Common Sea Snaxe

/k…ëmən si ssnaks/

Who?

The Common Sea Snaxe is an ordinary water-dwelling snaxe. This creature has gills and can therefore breathe water. This snaxe has the ability to spit water over long distances, which helps it stun and possibly kill both its predators and prey.

Evolution

About 36,000 years after the initial branching of the Snaxe Evolutionary Tree, the world started to heat up. The ice caps melted and sea levels rose. Some snaxii who lived in coastal areas had to deal with heavy rain and flooding and therefore developed adaptations to live underwater. The first of these water snaxii, the Generic Water Snaxe, was the direct ancestor of the Common Sea Snaxe.
